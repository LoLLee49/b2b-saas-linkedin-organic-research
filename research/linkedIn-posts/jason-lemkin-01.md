The hair salon picks up the phone for a $49 haircut

But most sales reps won't pick up an inbound call for a $5,000 deal, or even call them back the same day

One of The Great "Mysteries" of B2B sales
**Pattern**: Contrast → Absurd comparison → Expose contradiction → Force reflection
**Key Insight**: Lower-ticket businesses often outperform enterprise teams on one thing:

Speed of response.

The irony:

Pizza → immediate response → $19
Hair salon → immediate response → $49
B2B → forms, routing, qualification, follow-ups → potential $5,000+

The hidden question:

Why do businesses become harder to buy from as deal value increases?
**My Note**: The easier you make it to buy, the more you sell.Many B2B companies lose deals not because of product quality—but because of response friction.Small businesses act like every customer matters. Large sales teams sometimes act like customers can wait.
**Repurpose Angle**: ( Customer experience Angle )
Companies obsess over acquisition.

Customers obsess over responsiveness.

The first experience of your product is often not the demo.

It’s how long someone waited for a reply.
