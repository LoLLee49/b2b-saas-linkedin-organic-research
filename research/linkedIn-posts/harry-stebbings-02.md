**Key Insight**: Small acts of respect compound over long periods in invisible ways.
**Pattern**: Status → Human gesture → Emotional imprint → Time gap → Unexpected return
**Repurpose Angle**: Everyone talks about networking as collecting contacts.

But some of the strongest professional relationships start with someone noticing the quiet person in the waiting room.

You don’t always remember who opened doors.

You remember who opened the room.
**Full Text**:
10 years ago I was an intern at a fund. 

I remember the first morning, one of the managing partners saw me waiting in the waiting room and took 30 mins out for a coffee and to welcome me. 

Today, he pinged me after 8 years of not being in touch and asked for a favour for his daughter’s business. 

I did it without pause. Problem now solved. 

Karma is real. Be kind to the kid waiting in the wait room looking nervous.

**My Note**:
Contrarian angle

“Karma” may not even be the right word.

This is deferred trust.

People don’t repay kindness immediately.

They carry it until an opportunity appears.
