**Full Text**: Tony Fadell: "Every new product needs three generations to get right. You make the product, then you fix the product, then you fix the business." 

Even the iPod, it took three generations before it became successful.
**Pattern**: Authority → Counterintuitive principle → Rule of 3 → Iconic example → Universal lesson
**Key Insight**: Most people expect success to come from getting version one right.

But great products often emerge through three different optimization problems:

Make the product work → prove value
Make the product better → improve experience and fit
Make the business work → make it scalable and sustainable

The hidden lesson:

Iteration is not failure. Iteration is the business model of innovation.

People abandon Gen 1 too early because they mistake incomplete for incorrect.
**My Note**: This reframed something for me:

We often evaluate first attempts as final outcomes.

But many successful systems aren’t built once.

They evolve through stages.

Version 1 proves possibility.
Version 2 earns adoption.
Version 3 earns durability.

Maybe the question isn’t:

“Why isn’t this working yet?”

But:

“Which generation am I building?”
**Repurpose Angle**: (My own storytelling Angle)
People celebrate breakthrough moments.

History usually looks different:

Version 1: embarrassing.
Version 2: promising.
Version 3: obvious.

The world remembers version three.
The builder remembers all three.
(B2B / PM Angle)
Roadmaps fail when teams try to optimize:

UX
growth
monetization

all at once.

Different generations solve different problems.
