**Key Insight**:
In periods of major market change, winners don’t win by improving products — they win by **repositioning how the market understands them.**

**Pattern**:
Market shift framing → common mistake identification → positioning correction → strategic guidance → implicit category design lens

**Repurpose Angle (B2B SaaS)**:
When the market shifts (AI, new competitors, platform changes), don’t start with features. Start with **redefining what category you belong to in the customer’s mind.**

---

**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
April Dunford explains that during major market disruptions, most companies instinctively focus on updating messaging or adding features. However, this is usually the wrong level of response. Instead, she argues that the real opportunity lies in repositioning — changing how the market understands what your product is for, who it is for, and what alternatives it replaces. In fast-changing environments, old category assumptions break down, which creates space for companies to redefine their positioning and re-establish relevance. The post emphasizes that positioning is not just messaging work; it is a strategic lever that determines how buyers evaluate and compare solutions. Companies that fail to adjust positioning during market shifts risk being miscategorized or overlooked entirely, even if their product is strong.

---

**My Note**:
This is classic April Dunford thinking: **market change doesn’t automatically create winners — it creates a positioning vacuum, and whoever fills it first defines the category.**
