**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Melissa Perri argues that AI is creating a dangerous shift in product teams: speed is now being used as a justification to skip customer discovery. Because teams can build in weeks instead of months, there is a growing temptation to reduce or eliminate validation steps. She warns that this leads to a “build faster, fail faster” cycle where teams produce more output but not better outcomes. Historically, slower build cycles forced teams to validate ideas before committing resources. With AI removing that friction, organizations risk losing that natural checkpoint. However, she clarifies that AI can actually enhance discovery when used correctly — through faster analysis of customer feedback, rapid prototyping for validation, and broader experimentation. The core message is that the problem is not speed itself, but the loss of intentional learning cycles that ensure teams are building the right things.
