**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Kyle challenges the common belief that early customer targeting and pricing decisions are arbitrary or reversible. Using the well-known SaaS segmentation model (mice, rabbits, deer, elephants), he explains that your choice of customer segment fundamentally shapes your entire company trajectory. Small-ticket, high-volume models require massive distribution efficiency, while large-ticket enterprise models demand longer sales cycles and higher-touch relationships. The core message is that these paths are structurally different and difficult to switch between later. He also reinforces that as companies scale, the importance of picking the right ICP early becomes even more critical because it determines not just revenue potential, but also product design, GTM motion, and operational complexity. The post implicitly warns founders not to treat early ICP selection as reversible experimentation, but as a foundational commitment.

**My Note**:
This is Kyle’s “systems thinking” style: he reframes early GTM decisions as **path dependency**, meaning most SaaS failures are not execution failures — they are early structure mismatches that compound over time.
