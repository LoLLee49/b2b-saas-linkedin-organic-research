**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Ross Simmonds calls out a common mistake among social media managers: treating content publishing as a one-and-done action rather than a distribution opportunity. He emphasizes that platforms reward content that is actively shared and re-shared, not just initially posted. By underusing the “share” functionality and failing to intentionally redistribute existing content, teams leave significant reach and engagement potential untapped. The post reinforces his broader philosophy that content success is not just about creation, but about building systems for continuous distribution and amplification. Instead of constantly chasing new content, teams should maximize the lifespan and reach of existing assets through structured sharing behavior.
