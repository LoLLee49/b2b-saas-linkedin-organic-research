**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Brian Balfour reflects on recent work shipped at Reforge, emphasizing that the most meaningful outputs are not always the ones users directly see. Instead, he highlights that a large portion of impactful work lies in improving internal systems, workflows, and processes that compound over time. These include better ways of structuring experimentation, improving how teams learn from outputs, and strengthening the underlying operating system that supports product and growth work. The post suggests that teams often over-index on visible features while underestimating the importance of foundational improvements that enable faster and higher-quality execution in the future. The core idea is that sustainable performance is driven less by isolated wins and more by compounding system improvements that make every future cycle more effective than the last.
