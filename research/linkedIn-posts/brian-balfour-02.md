**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Brian Balfour analyzes the announcement of Claude Code to Figma as evidence of a deeper shift in how users begin product workflows. Traditionally, design tools like Figma sat at the beginning of the product creation process. However, with AI coding tools like Claude Code becoming capable of generating functional interfaces, users are now starting their work inside code-first or AI-first environments instead of design-first tools. Balfour argues that this shift is not temporary — AI will only get better at exploring variations, reducing friction, and accelerating early-stage ideation. As a result, tools like Figma must adapt by embedding themselves into these upstream environments or risk losing relevance. The core strategic implication is that products must not only optimize their own user experience, but also ensure they remain part of the user's *initial decision path*. If users begin elsewhere, traditional product boundaries start to dissolve.
