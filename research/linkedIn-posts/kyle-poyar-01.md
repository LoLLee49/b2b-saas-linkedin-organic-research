**Full Text (summary, not verbatim)**:
Kyle Poyar outlines a shift in how modern GTM teams operate. Instead of relying on static “plays” like outbound campaigns or re-engagement sequences, leading teams are building AI-powered systems that continuously run in the background. These systems combine data, automation, and AI agents to detect signals (like customer churn risk, competitor movement, or buying intent), trigger workflows automatically, and support multi-step GTM execution without manual coordination. He highlights how tools like CRM systems, enrichment platforms, and AI agents now connect into a unified GTM stack, enabling workflows such as closed-lost reactivation, competitor displacement campaigns, champion tracking, and automated prospect research before meetings. However, he also emphasizes boundaries: not everything should be automated. High-trust actions like customer communication, ICP judgment, and strategic interpretation still require humans. The core idea is balance — automate repeatable execution, preserve human judgment for decisions.

**My Note**:
This post is a structural evolution of GTM thinking: it moves from “growth tactics” to “operating systems.” The real message is not automation — it’s **coordination at scale through AI orchestration layers.**
